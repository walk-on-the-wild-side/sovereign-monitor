# Issue template (locked — Track A1)

> Copy this file to `issues/YYYY-MM-DD.md`, fill it in, and paste into Substack.
> Section order is locked; every issue ships every section. AI assistance may
> summarize data deltas and draft prose from the issue pack; a human verifies every
> number and signs off before publishing (SPEC: publishing workflow).

---

## 1. Headline read (~150 words)

One paragraph: the single most important sovereign-stress development this week
across the coverage set, and why it matters. Written last, after the data is in.

## 2. What moved — spreads & FX (table)

Paste the `what_moved.csv` table from the issue pack. Columns: country, FX vs USD
(level, 1-week %, YTD %), EM OAS proxy (level, 1-week change), reserves context
(months of imports, latest year). One sentence under the table naming the biggest
mover and the likely driver.

## 3. Country spotlight (1–2, ~200 words each)

Deeper read on one or two countries where something changed: what moved, the
structural backdrop (debt profile, creditor mix, program status), what to watch
next. Cite every claim with a link.

## 4. The empire / war / culture lens (~150 words)

The week's geopolitics as it bears on sovereign balance sheets: China-sphere
lending and influence, conflict spillovers, sanctions, domestic politics. Links to
reporting (OCCRP, regional outlets, wire headlines) — never reproduced text.

## 5. Chart of the week

Embed `chart_of_week.png` from the issue pack. One caption sentence stating what
the reader should see. The attribution line is part of the image.

## 6. Sourced links

Bulleted list of every article/report referenced: `outlet — headline (link)`.
Headlines and links only; our own one-line summary where useful.
